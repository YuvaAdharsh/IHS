Abraham's Journey

Following the patriarch's path through the ancient Near East (c. 2166-1991 BCE)

Key Locations in Abraham's Life

Dating Note: These dates follow traditional biblical chronology. Modern scholarship suggests Abraham may have lived during the Middle Bronze Age (c. 2100-1550 BCE), with some placing him closer to 2000-1800 BCE based on archaeological evidence.
Ur of the Chaldeans - Southern Iraq
Abraham's birthplace in ancient Mesopotamia (c. 2166 BCE)
Haran - Southeastern Turkey
Where Abraham's family settled and where he received God's call (departed c. 2091 BCE)
Canaan - Palestine/Israel
The Promised Land, including Hebron, Bethel, and Beersheba (c. 2091-1991 BCE)
Egypt - Egypt
Temporary refuge during famine in Canaan (c. 2080s BCE)
Mecca (Kaaba) - Saudi Arabia
Islamic tradition: Abraham and Ishmael built the Kaaba here (c. 2070s BCE)

Abraham's Journey Timeline (Most Accepted Dates)

1
Born in Ur (c. 2166 BCE) - Ancient Sumerian city in Mesopotamia
Traditional biblical chronology places his birth around this time
2
Moved to Haran (c. 2091 BCE) - Family migration to northern Mesopotamia
Age 75 when called to leave for Canaan (Genesis 12:4)
3
Called to Canaan (c. 2091 BCE) - Divine command to leave Haran for the Promised Land
Beginning of the covenant promises
4
Sojourn in Egypt (c. 2080s BCE) - Temporary stay during famine
Brief period shortly after arriving in Canaan
5
Isaac Born (c. 2066 BCE) - Son of promise born when Abraham was 100
Fulfillment of covenant promise (Genesis 21:5)
6
Built the Kaaba (c. 2070s BCE) - Islamic tradition: With Ishmael in Mecca
Quran 2:127 - "And [mention] when Abraham was raising the foundations of the House"
7
Death in Canaan (c. 1991 BCE) - Died at age 175, buried in Cave of Machpelah
Traditional chronology based on Genesis 25:7
